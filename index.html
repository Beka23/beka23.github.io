My Background

My full name is Beksultan, but my friends usually call me Bek for short. I'm 23. When I was 18, I moved to the US from Kyrgyzstan to pursue a career in software engineering. I went to NOVA community college in Annandale, Virginia. My major was computer science. After two years of studying English, general education, and some CS, I had to move to NYC due to personal issues. Here in the City, I worked at the moving company, as a mover first, then as an assistant operational manager. At this job, I learned how to work under pressure, be self-organized, and adapt to fast-changing environments. These skills are really helpful in software engineering and I'm glad that I was able to learn them. I always wanted to go back to college, but I found out about coding bootcamps. I did research on some bootcamps, and found Flatiron School, one of the best coding bootcamps in the US. I really liked the tech-stack that the school is teaching, especially JavaScript and React JS, so I decided to attend Flatiron School. At Flatiron, I enhanced my back-end and front-end skills. Learned how to work with databases, interactive designs, and other essential features of modern web applications. I keep improving my coding skills every day, I like to learn new things, new technologies, and frameworks. I think coding is cool and I truly love it. This is probably something that I would do for the rest of my life. In my free time, I like to play soccer, go for a run or watch some sports. I'm open to all the opportunities, so feel free to get in touch!
